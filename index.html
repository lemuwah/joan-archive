Who Was Joan? — Unknown
Open-access research library · Joan, wife of John Greene of Quidnessett

The Joan Archive

In 1682, a deed dividing John Greene's Quidnessett lands requires the consent of Joan, his wife — "or to her mother if she survive." We have no baptism, marriage, or burial record for Joan before or after this deed. Her mother is not named anywhere.

She does not appear in church rolls, court testimony, probate, or gravestones. Early Rhode Island historians (Arnold, Austin, Huling) list her only as "wife Joan/e" with no further detail. That absence is the archive's subject.

Thesis (hostile-reviewer safe): Joan Quidnessett = unknown. "Joan Beggarly" has no primary source support and is chronologically impossible — Alice Beggerly died Jan 1643/44 (Surgeon John of Warwick, Winthrop Papers Vol.4) while Joan appears 1682. Anashuecot (1672 "Chiefe Sachem and heire") is the closest documented land-holding sachem for the same tract and is kept as a geographic comparator only — not an identity claim. Our focus is digitization, not assertion.

Places Joan/e should appear, but does not

  • Missing Church rolls — First Baptist Providence, Newport, no Joan/e Greene c.1640–1682.
  • Missing Court testimony — Women of comparable status appear as witnesses. Joan/e never does.
  • Missing Probate / gravestone — No will, inventory, or stone.
  • Present 1682 home-place deed only — Sole primary source appearance.

History is rotting on the shelf — the answer might be in a box that hasn't been photographed

Joan isn't "lost to history." She is undigitized. Original land evidence sits on vinegar-decay microfilm. The paper that could name her is actively decaying in basements across three continents. Better access might hold the answer.

What We Don't Know

The honest gaps — no smoothing

When did John Greene die?

1685 deed implies death by 25 Sep 1685. Austin (1887) favors c.1695 citing a will. No will survives. Both presented; 1685 weighted higher.

Who was Joan's mother?

"Or to her mother if she survive" implies mother living 1682. No mother identified anywhere. If Joan held land rights, mother might be of interest — unknown.

1676 Newport Court Martial transcript

John Greene denies charge; son Daniel testifies for prosecution. Father-son split atypical. Hough 1858 verbatim pending — highest priority digitization.
