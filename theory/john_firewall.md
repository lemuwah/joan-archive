# John Firewall

Tracks all distinct "John Greene" figures across Rhode Island, Warwick, Quidnessett, and related lines to prevent generational merges, floating legends, and misattributed identities.

## 1. Core Principle

The John Firewall prevents:
- Collapsing multiple Johns into one
- Misassigning deeds, affidavits, or wartime roles
- Using secondary genealogies to override primary evidence
- Floating legends (“born Clarke”) from contaminating the Quidnessett line

## 2. Primary John Greene Figures

### 2.1 John Greene Sr. of Quidnessett
- **Status:** 🟢 PROVEN
- **Key evidence:** 1679 affidavit (“forty years and more…”)
- **Location:** Cocumscussoc / Quidnessett
- **Notes:** Central figure; husband of Joan; father of Daniel², James², Henry², Lieut. John², Benjamin², Sarah².

### 2.2 Lieut. John² Greene (son)
- **Status:** 🟢 PROVEN
- **Key evidence:** 1694 Massachusetts probate
- **Notes:** Distinct from father; appears in Bates’s sibling list.

### 2.3 “Capt. John Greene” (Warwick line)
- **Status:** 🟢 PROVEN (distinct line)
- **Key evidence:** Wartime advisory council
- **Notes:** Firewall rule: NOT the Quidnessett John; separate Warwick identity.

### 2.4 East Greenwich John (“born Clarke” legend)
- **Status:** ⚪ UNVERIFIED
- **Key evidence:** Secondary genealogies; floating legend
- **Notes:** Legend attaches independently to ≥2 Greene lines; cannot be merged.

## 3. Conflation Risks

### 3.1 Generational Merge (Sr. vs. Jr.)
- **Risk:** Treating John Sr. and Lieut. John² as one person.
- **Firewall rule:** Bates’s thesis holds — John Sr. used two place-based names across his own lifetime; NOT a generational merge.

### 3.2 Warwick vs. Quidnessett
- **Risk:** Assigning Warwick deeds or wartime roles to Quidnessett John.
- **Firewall rule:** Distinct lines; no crossover without primary proof.

### 3.3 “Born Clarke” floating legend
- **Risk:** Legend used to justify merges or identity shifts.
- **Firewall rule:** Legend treated as pattern, not evidence.
