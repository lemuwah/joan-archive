## 1. Executive Summary & Purpose

The year 1682 marks the only point in the surviving documentary record where Joan Greene appears by her English name in direct legal actions. 

Rather than viewing these deeds solely as genealogical markers, this matrix reconstructs the **administrative lifecycle** of the 1682 property transactions. It details how English land allocation, dower rights, and life-estate encumbrances were used to settle Quidnessett property among sons Daniel and James while legally securing Joan's maintenance and title releases.

## 3. Node-by-Node Detailed Breakdown

### Node 1: Estate Partition & Pre-Transaction Context (Early 1682)
* **Administrative Context:** John Greene Sr., approaching the end of his life (deceased before Sept 1685), sought to partition his 180-acre home-place in Quidnessett among his sons without subjecting the land to probate dispute or invalidating dower rights.
* **Legal Friction:** Under 17th-century English law, a wife held customary dower rights (a life estate in one-third of real property). To convey clear title to sons, the instrument had to either explicitly preserve her life estate or record her voluntary release.

---

### Node 2: Home-Place Deed Execution & Life-Estate Clause (24 March 1681/82)
* **Administrative Context:** Execution of the primary division instrument conveying 180 acres total: 120 acres to Daniel Greene and 60 acres to James Greene.
* **The Encumbrance / Life-Estate Clause:**
  * Contains the explicit protective language securing parental maintenance: *"as long as their father or mother should live"* / *"or to her mother if she survive"*.
* **Legal Function:** Converts an immediate fee-simple transfer into a **remainder interest**, ensuring Joan retained legal right of occupancy and support on the homestead if John predeceased her.
* **Source Anchor:** F.L. Greene (*Descendants of Joseph Greene of Westerly*, 1894, p. 10; transcript from NK Land Evidence).
* **Status:** **PROVEN.**

---

### Node 3: Pawtuxet Sale Consent & Independent Legal Agency (19 May 1682)
* **Administrative Context:** Sale of Pawtuxet land rights to William Carpenter for £10.
* **Scribal & Legal Action:**
  * Explicitly records Joan’s *"free & voluntary consent"* to the land transfer.
  * Formally signs with *"her marke"*.
* **Legal Significance:** Demonstrates independent legal agency required under Rhode Island colony law for dower releases, proving she was alive, recognized by colony magistrates, and capable of executing legal consent.
* **Source Anchor:** *Records of the Colony of Rhode Island and Providence Plantations*, Vol. VII, pp. 177–178.
* **Status:** **PROVEN.**

---

### Node 4: Magisterial Acknowledgment & Enrolment (1682)
* **Administrative Context:** Presentation before a colony magistrate for acknowledgment and recording in colony/town evidence books.
* **Source Anchor:** Records of RI Colony, Vol. VII; North Kingstown Land Evidence.
* **Documented Gap (GAP 1):** Direct examination of the physical North Kingstown Land Evidence volume (badly damaged in the 1869 town hall fire) to check for surviving uncopied marginalia or additional witness marks on the 1682 partition.

---

### Node 5: Death of Settlor & Life-Estate Activation (Pre-25 September 1685)
* **Administrative Context:** John Greene Sr. dies prior to 25 September 1685, activating the life-estate clause and vesting full management of the reserved homestead rights in Joan (if surviving).
* **Source Anchor:** Louise Prosser Bates (*RIHS Collections* XI, 1918), citing primary deed of 25 Sept 1685 explicitly referencing *"John Greene Sr. deceased"*.
* **Status:** **PROVEN** (Disproves all post-1685 death dates for John Sr.).

---

### Node 6: Post-Mortem Possession & Dower Rights (1685–1690s)
* **Administrative Context:** Daniel and James Greene hold title subject to their mother’s life encumbrance.
* **Documented Gap (GAP 2):** Absence of a formal probate or administration record for Joan Greene upon her eventual death. Under RI colonial practice, unprobated widow life-estates quietly merged into the sons' remainder titles without creating a separate estate inventory unless contested.

---

### Node 7: Subsequent Chain of Title (Daniel & James Division)
* **Administrative Context:** Subsequent sales and devises by Daniel and James Greene off the 180-acre tract.
* **Source Anchor:** North Kingstown Land Evidence (Deeds of Daniel Greene to his heirs; James Greene land transactions).
* **Status:** **PROBABLE.**

---

### Node 8: Cartographic Anchorage — 1717/18 Quidnessett Plat Map
* **Administrative Context:** The 1717/18 survey of Quidnessett preserves the boundaries laid down in the 1672 purchase and 1682 division.
* **Map Annotation:** Identifies the parcel as *"John Green & Son 151 Ac"*, confirming the geographic continuity of the family homestead through the second generation.
* **Source Anchor:** Rhode Island State Archives (1717/18 Quidnessett Plat).
* **Status:** **PROVEN.**

---

### Node 9: Archival Storage & Modern Publishing History
* **1894:** Frank L. Greene publishes transcript of the 1682 Home-Place Deed in *Descendants of Joseph Greene of Westerly*.
* **1918:** Louise Prosser Bates publishes the 1685 death anchor in *RIHS Collections*.
* **Present Storage:** Rhode Island State Archives; North Kingstown Town Clerk Vault.
