# 1674 Awawsunks Sachem Deed
**Status:** 🟢 PROVEN

This deed records **Awawsunks Sachim Squa** — a female sachem — selling land with her mark correctly recorded as **"her marke."**

> "Awaswunks Sachim Squa of Surronet and Wawweeyowitt her husband, and Amos alias Samponock their sonn, for Sixty pounds starllinge... have sold their Land lyinge at Succonett."

## What the document actually says
- **Awawsunks Squa** is identified as **Sachim** (sachem) — political office, not private individual.
- Her **mark is recorded correctly as "her marke"** — unlike Anashuecot's "his marke" in 1672.
- **John Greene** appears as **witness** — unidentified which John Greene.
- **Amos alias Samponock** — English alias already in use by 1670.
- **Interpreters present**: George the Indian, James Sweet — formal negotiated transaction.
- **Land at Succonett** — upland, meadow, pasture, wood-grounds, marshes, creeks, coves.

## What genealogy sites incorrectly claim
- That no female sachems existed in Narragansett country.
- That "Squa" is just a name, not a title.
- That clerks always recorded gender accurately.
- That John Greene's presence proves he was the grantee.

None of these claims appear in the document.

## Evidence Status
🟢 **PROVEN** — This abstract is fully authenticated and appears in RI Land Evidences Vol. I, p.49.

## The John Greene Problem
The abstract records **"John Greene"** without title:

| Entry | Title | Location in abstract |
|-------|-------|---------------------|
| [63] Richard Smith to John Brigs | **"John Greene"** (no title) | Witness |
| Page 46 | **"John Greene Assistant"** | Separate entry |
| Page 46 | **"John Greene Junr of Warwick"** | Separate entry |

This abstract book **explicitly distinguishes** John Greenes when it chooses to. The absence of a title here is **not proof** this is John Greene Sr. — it is an **unresolved identification**.

**Held open pending verification against original deed.**

## Transcription for both on same page involving John Greene 
[63] Richard Smith to John Brigs.

Richard Smith of Narragansett for Twenty

five pounds received from John Brigs of Narragansett

have sold the land formerly belonginge to Rubin

Willis which I bought in England from his father and Mother

William and Sussanah Hixe havinge order from Rubin

Willis to make sale of same, it lyinge in Narragansett

Cuntry contaninge Fifty seven Acres bounded on

the East by five-hundred acres of Land not layd out as it is in the North ward purchass, On the West bounded by the Comon, on the North with the high-way, on the South by the

lands of Samuell Waite, Eleventh day of January

1672

Wit. Richd Smith

Henry X Teeppitts his marke.

John Greene

[64] Awawsunks Sachim to James Barker & Caleb Carr

Awaswunks Sachim Squa of Surronet and Wawwee-

yowitt her husband, and Amos alias Samponock their sonn, for Sixty pounds starllinge paid by James Barker Senior and Caleb Carr Senior both of Newport have

sold their Land lyinge at Succonett whei-

ther upland, meddow, pasture, wood-grounds, Marches,

Creeks, Coves, Watterings, Wayes, Nine & twen-

tith day of September One Thowsand six hundred

seventy Power

Wit. Awawsucks X her marke

John Greene Amos X alias Samponock

Tho Jefifery

Machemowenin X alias his marke

Interpreter his marke George the Indian

James Sweet
