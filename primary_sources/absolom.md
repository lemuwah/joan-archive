# 1677/8 Absolom Affidavit
**Status:** 🟡 PROBABLE

This affidavit records **Absolom**, identified as a **"Counsellor"** (a political office, not a private individual), testifying before Peleg Sanford at Newport on **3 January 1677/8** (Old Style/New Style).

&gt; "Appeared before me an Indian called Absolom and Declared as followeth... he being carried out of the country... Browne left as a guardian by his father Tocomminion... James should not deprive him of his lands."

## What the document actually says

- **Absolom is a political actor**: He is labeled "Counsellor" above his mark — a title, not a personal descriptor.
- **Anashuecot / Awashuecutt "conveyed out of the country"**: The key phrase. Departure, captivity, or displacement during/after King Philip's War. The record does **not** say she died.
- **Father: Tocomminon**: Named explicitly as Anashuecot's father.
- **Guardian: Mr. Browne**: Left as guardian by Tocomminion — a protective/legal arrangement between Narragansett leadership and English settlers.
- **Brother referenced**: A "James" (or a son named James) is mentioned in the context of land deprivation; "Washaucutt" appears in related Fones Record entries as brother.
- **Land transaction witnessed**: Absolom attests he was present when Moseupp (the sachem) made a bargain and sale of land to **John Greene, Henry Tibbitts, John Stone, Thomas [?], and John Briggs**.
- **Date of original sale**: "The first day of January 1671" [i.e., 1671/2] — this refers back to the 1672 Fones Purchase Deed.
- **Signers reproduced at top of page**: The oval at the top of p. 22 copies the signature block from the 1672 deed: **Anashuecot, Wampkegge, Ompamiatt, Nammeash, Seecomp, Tyecuecsha** — all marked "his marke."

## What genealogy sites incorrectly claim

- That Absolom was an English settler or interpreter.  
  **FALSE.** The document explicitly identifies him as "an Indian called Absolom."
- That Anashuecot died in 1676.  
  **UNVERIFIED.** This document says "conveyed out of the country" — not death. The 1676 court-martial references "Anaftiawin" (a phonetic variant) and does not record execution.
- That no Indigenous political office of "Counsellor" existed.  
  **FALSE.** The title appears directly above Absolom's mark.
- That the 1672 deed and 1677/8 affidavit were entered contemporaneously.  
  **FALSE.** Both were entered into the Fones Record on **24 July 1703** — 31 years after the affidavit, 31 years after the deed. Clerk Fones was retroactively building a paper trail for Rhode Island's land claim.

## Evidence Status

🟡 **PROBABLE** — Primary source attestation, but entered 31 years post-execution (1703). Isolated from the main 1686 Fones campaign. The testimony itself is contemporary (1677/8), but the surviving copy is a late transcription. Chronological vulnerability cuts both ways.

## The John Greene Problem

The affidavit records **"John Greene"** without title or generational marker:

| Entry | Context | Identification |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| 1672 Fones Deed | Grantee/purchaser | **Unverified** — could be Sr., Jr., or another John Greene entirely |
| 1674 Awawsunks Deed | Witness | **Unverified** — no title given |
| 1677/8 Absolom Affidavit | Named as purchaser | **Unverified** — "John Greene" bare, no "Sr.," "Jr.," or "of Warwick" |

The Five-John Firewall holds: this document **does not** resolve which John Greene was present. It only confirms a Greene was a party to the 1671/2 land transaction attested by Absolom.

**Held open pending verification against original deed.**

**[Main text — Absolom Affidavit, Fones Record, p. 22]**

Newport on Rhod Island January 3d 1677

Appeared before me an Indian called Absolom and Declared as followeth  
that the Indian Sacham Moseupp an Indian [...] a bargaine and saile  
of land that the Indian Sacham made unto John Greene Henry Tibbitts  
John Stone Thomas [...] and John Briggs and was present at the time  
of the [...] of the Deed of saile for the same and when possession  
[...] taken, and that the severall Bounds and lines of the said  
[...] was Truly Explained unto the Sacham Awashuecutt as it was  
[...] of Sayle haveing Date the first day of January 1671 [...]  
and that the severall [...] of the said [...] Sayle Expresse was  
to Run straight only the line of the [...] River and further saith  
that Awashuecutt his two [...] sons allso [...] the said lands and  
sett there hand [...] unto which I am a witnes the said Absolom  
[...] he thanked god he had this opportunity to Declare [...]  
[he] being carried out of the country [...] by his sonnes not  
honestly could any part of it before [...] Browne left as a guardian  
by his father Tocomminion [...] James should not deprive him of  
his lands this at [...] as the Indian spoke.

Before Peleg Sanford Assistant

[... aged about thirty one years upon his ...]  
[...] the 3d January 167[7/8]
