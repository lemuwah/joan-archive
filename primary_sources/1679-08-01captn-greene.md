## Context
General Assembly vote following the return of agents Holden and Greene from England. The colony reimburses them for expenses incurred while lobbying the Crown regarding Narragansett jurisdiction (the King's Province dispute).

## Hard Facts Established
- John Greene held the rank of Captain.
- He traveled to England as a colonial agent.
- He handled colony funds in London and was formally reimbursed by legislative order.
- He was partnered with Randall Holden in high-level colonial diplomacy.

## Limitations
- Does not specify Greene's activities in England beyond "disbursed... on the Colony's account."
- Does not name any family members.
- Does not establish Greene's precise relationship to the Quidnessett line (though the Holden association makes it probable).
- The £60 appropriation was to be raised by town assessments; we do not know if Greene was ever actually paid.
