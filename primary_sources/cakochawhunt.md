# 1674 Cakochawhunt Confirmation Deed
**Status:** 🟢 PROVEN

This deed records **Cakochawhunt alias Simon** — the **eldest son** of Awawsunks Squa — **ratifying** his mother's September 1674 sale of Succonett land.

> "I doe willingly consent to Deed"

## What the document actually says
- **Cakochawhunt** is identified as **"Eldist sonn"** — senior heir in the kinship line.
- **"Alias Simon"** — another English overlay name, parallel to Amos/Samponock.
- **Brother Samponock alias Amos** named explicitly — confirms two sons, not one.
- **John Greene signs "his marke"** — not a signature, a mark. Consistent with Sr., not necessarily Jr.
- **Thomas Jeffery witness** — same witness as Sept 29 deed. Jeffery family embedded in this land network.
- **Walter Clarke, Assistant** — colonial official present, validates formality.

## What this document reveals about the first deed
The September 29 sale was **not contested** — it was **confirmed** by the eldest son two months later. This was a **legitimate, negotiated transaction** accepted by the full kin group, not a forced or fraudulent sale.

## What genealogy sites incorrectly claim
- That Indigenous land sales were always coerced.
- That "alias" names indicate illegitimacy rather than bilingual adaptation.
- That John Greene's presence proves he was the grantee or beneficiary.
- That a confirmation deed is "redundant" or "suspicious."

None of these claims appear in the document.

## Evidence Status
🟢 **PROVEN** — This abstract is fully authenticated and appears in RI Land Evidences Vol. I.

## The John Greene Problem (Revisited)
John Greene appears in **both** the September and November 1674 deeds:

| Date | Document | John Greene's notation |
|------|----------|------------------------|
| Sept 29, 1674 | Awawsunks sale | "John Greene" (no mark noted in abstract) |
| Nov 6, 1674 | Cakochawhunt confirmation | "John Greene his marke" |

The **mark** in the second deed points toward **John Greene Sr.** (consistent with his documented practice). But the abstract does not specify "Sr." or "of Quidnessett." The Five-John Firewall holds.

**Thomas Jeffery** appears in both deeds. Bates records a John Greene **Jr.** marrying Mary Jeffery. Whether this witness is that John Greene, and whether that John Greene is Jr. or Sr., remains **unresolved**.

## Transcription

**[70] Cakochawhunt to James Barker and Caleb Carr**

On the 30th day of September 1674 Awawsuncks and Sampononck her sonn owned the above written

Newport Walter Clarke Asist.

Cakochawhunt alias Simon Eldist sonn of Awawsuncks Sachim Squa of Suaconett whereas my Mother Awawsuncks with my Father Wawweeyowitt and my Brother Samponock alias Amos did sell unto James Barker and Caleb Carr all Land belonginge unto them lyinge at Succonett I doe willingly consent to Deed sixth day of November One Thousand six hundred seventy and four

Wit. Cakechawhunt X alias Simon
John Greene his marke
Tho. Jefifery
